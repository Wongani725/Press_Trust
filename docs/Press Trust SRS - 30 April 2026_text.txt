3. Introduction
Press Trust Malawi is a charitable trust established to promote social and economic
development in Malawi, with a strong focus on supporting access to education through
scholarships and related educational support programmes. The Trust operates as an
independent, non-political institution committed to equity, transparency, accountability,
and national development impact.
The Scholarship Management System (SMS) is intended to digitise and streamline Press
Trust’s scholarship administration processes. The system will provide a single, authoritative
digital platform to manage the full scholarship lifecycle, from application intake,
administration, disbursement oversight, beneficiary monitoring, and reporting. By digitising
these processes, Press Trust aims to improve service delivery, reduce manual processing
risks, strengthen governance and auditability, and enhance data-driven decisionmaking.
4. Purpose
The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) is to define a complete,
clear, and unambiguous set of functional and non-functional requirements for the
Scholarship Management System (SMS). This document serves as the authoritative
baseline for system design, development, testing, deployment, and acceptance. It is
intended to enable software vendors and developers to deliver the SMS with minimal
clarification cycles, thereby reducing implementation risk, cost overruns, and project
delays.
5. Scope
The Scholarship Management System (SMS) will support Press Trust’s scholarship operations
from program setup through application intake, approvals, disbursement tracking,
beneficiary monitoring, and reporting. The system will provide portals for applicants,
external stakeholders, and internal workflows for Press Trust operations, finance, M&E,
management, and administrators.
6. Background and core functions of Press Trust Malawi
Press Trust Malawi is a charitable trust with a national mandate to provide funding for
social and economic development in Malawi, including education support and other
national benefit purposes. Press Trust’s operational core functions include:
• Scholarship management: administering merit- and/or needs-based education
support, including selection, payments, and beneficiary tracking.
• Stakeholder management: working with schools/institutions, guardians,
donors/sponsors, and implementing partners.
• Monitoring, evaluation, and learning: tracking performance, outcomes, and impact
metrics for funded beneficiaries and programs.
• Financial stewardship and reporting: ensuring traceable approvals and payments,
compliance, and timely reporting to management, board, and stakeholders.
7. Overall Description
7.1 Product perspective
The Scholarship Management System (SMS) must be a web-based information system
(with optional mobile-first design) that centralizes scholarship data, workflows,
documents, decisions, and financial tracking. It will replace the manual and spreadsheetbased processes and provides controlled access to internal and external users.
7.2 Product functions
At a high level, the SMS shall provide the following capabilities:
• Program configuration and publishing (program setup, forms, rules, timelines,
templates).
• Applicant portal (registration, application, document upload, tracking, notifications).
• Eligibility screening and validation (automated and manual overrides with audit trail).
• Entitlements allocation and Inventory management.
• Award management (letters/agreements, acceptance, renewals, lifecycle status).
• Disbursement and financial tracking (schedules, approvals, payment evidence,
reconciliation, exceptions).
• Beneficiary monitoring and case management (performance, site visits, interventions,
outcomes).
• Reporting and analytics (dashboards, exports, custom reports, dynamic reports).
• Administration and security (RBAC, audit logging, configuration without code, data
protection).

7.4 Operating environment
The SMS must operate as a secure web application accessible over modern browsers
(Chrome/Edge/Firefox) and optimized for mobile devices. The solution may be deployed
on cloud infrastructure or on premises, subject to procurement decisions. The system shall
support low-bandwidth usage through optimized pages and resumable uploads.
The system shall support deployment on industry-standard operating systems, including
low-cost, open-source platforms, with full vendor or enterprise support.
7.5 Hardware
The solution must be capable of operating on either virtualized environments (including
on-premise or cloud-based virtual machines) or bare-metal servers. The proposed
architecture should be scalable to accommodate future growth in users, data volumes,
and transaction loads without significant hardware reconfiguration.
7.6 Software / Operating System
The solution must support deployment on industry-standard operating systems. Use of lowcost, open-source platforms is acceptable, and licensing should not restrict flexibility in
operating system choice.
7.7 Database
The solution must support a robust, secure, and proven relational database management
system (RDBMS). Acceptable databases may include MySQL, PostgreSQL, Oracle
Database, or Microsoft SQL Server, or equivalent enterprise-grade databases. The
database should support high availability, data integrity, backup and recovery, and
scalability.
7.8 Application Software
The system must be web-based, accessible through standard web browsers without the
need for specialized client installations. The application shall utilize open data exchange
and interoperability standards (such as RESTful APIs, XML, or JSON) to facilitate integration
with third-party systems and future platforms.
8. Assumptions and dependencies
• Press Trust will provide or approve official program policies for configuration.
• Integration with SAGE Evolution, Email Gateway.
• The solution must support hosting at Press Trust offices or any other approved hosting
environment/site, without technical or licensing limitations.
• User onboarding will include training and agreed support processes.
• Where historical records are available and of acceptable quality, the proposed
solution shall support the migration of up to five (5) years of data from existing Excelbased records into the new system.
9. Functional Requirements
This section provides detailed functional requirements organized by capability area.
9.1 Core Functional Requirements
9.1.1 Applicant and Beneficiary Management
The system shall support the registration and management of applicants and
beneficiaries throughout the Scholarship lifecycle. This module shall include:
• Beneficiary biodata and demographic information
• Academic and institutional details
• Linking beneficiaries to schools and academic periods
• Maintenance of schools and institutions master data, including school names,
types, districts, locations, contact details, and bank/payment details
• Status management (applicant, active beneficiary, suspended, completed,
terminated)
• Secure storage of supporting documentation
9.1.2 Application and Eligibility Management
The system shall support the end-to-end management of Scholarship applications,
including:
• Configurable application forms for new and renewal applications
• Application tracking and status updates
9.1.3 Award and Disbursement Management
The system shall support the management of Scholarship awards:
• Award creation, amounts, duration, and conditions
• Payments made directly to schools (tuition, boarding, exam fees, school shoes,
school uniform, books and other charges)
• Disbursement schedules, payment tracking, and reconciliation
• Support management, including:
o Recording of items purchased by the Press Trust
o Issuance of items to beneficiaries
o Valuation of issued items
o Capture and storage of beneficiary acknowledgement and signatures
confirming receipt
• Linkage of awards and disbursements to funding sources or sponsors.
9.1.4 Monitoring, Reporting, and Audit
The system shall support governance, oversight, and accountability through:
• Monitoring academic progress and compliance with Scholarship conditions.
• Tracking of sponsor-funded and Trust-funded beneficiaries.
• Standard and ad-hoc reporting by beneficiary, school, district, sponsor, and
period.
• Budget utilization and commitment reporting.
• Comprehensive system audit logs and reporting.
• The system must capture academic progress and milestone reports, to assess
continued eligibility.
• The system must trigger alerts for non-compliance or poor performance, to
enable early intervention.
• The system must support continuation, suspension, or termination decisions, to
protect funding integrity.
10. Non-core functional requirements
10.1.1 Document & Records Management
• The system must provide secure centralized document storage (to preserve
institutional records).
• The system must support version control and access permissions (to prevent
unauthorized changes).
• The system must enable advanced search and retrieval (to improve operational
efficiency).
10.1.2 Communication & Notifications
• The system must send automated email notifications (To keep applicants and
staff informed).
• The system must support bulk communications and templates (To manage large
applicant volumes efficiently).
• The system must log all communication activities (To ensure traceability).
10.1.3 Reporting & Analytics
• The system must generate real-time operational dashboards (to support
management oversight)
• The system must produce donor and compliance reports (to meet funding
requirements).
• The system must support data export in standard formats (PDF, Excel, CSV) - (to
facilitate analysis and audits).
10.1.4 System Configuration & Administration
• The system must allow configuration of workflows, forms, and notifications without
coding (to ensure flexibility and sustainability).
• The system must support backup and disaster recovery mechanisms (to ensure
business continuity).
• The system must provide system performance monitoring tools (to maintain
uptime and reliability).
10.1.5 User & Access Management
• The system must support role-based access control (RBAC) – (to ensure users only
access functions relevant to their responsibilities).
• The system must allow secure user registration and authentication (To protect
applicant and institutional data).
• The system must maintain full audit logs of user activities (to support
accountability, investigations, and compliance audits).
• The system must enforce password and security policies (to reduce unauthorized
access risks).

14.1 Security and privacy
• Authentication shall support strong passwords, configurable lockout after 3 failed
attempts, and MFA for all users.
• The system shall ensure that user logins are securely managed and that users can only
view or access information they are authorized to see.
• Sessions shall be securely managed and terminated on logout; browser back
navigation shall not restore authenticated pages.
• Audit logs shall capture security events and all key business actions; logs shall be
tamper-evident and exportable for audit.
• File uploads shall be virus-scanned; file size limits shall be configurable (recommended
50–100MB per file unless policy requires more).
• The system shall ensure that all data exchanged over the network is encrypted using
a secure SSL/TLS certificate.
• The system shall support field-level access control to restrict viewing of sensitive fields
(e.g., bank account numbers, national IDs) based on user role.
• The system shall log all data export activities, including user, date/time, report name,
and file format.
• The system shall enforce segregation of duties between disbursement creation and
disbursement approval.
• The system shall support configurable data retention periods and secure archival of
inactive records.
• All API endpoints shall require secure authentication and authorization.
• The system shall log all access to sensitive data fields, including user, timestamp, and
action performed.
• The system shall enforce approval workflow for changes to stored bank account
details.
• The system shall retain audit logs for a configurable period in accordance with
organizational policy.
• Audit logs shall be protected from modification or deletion by unauthorized users.
• Access to audit log configuration and management shall be restricted to authorized
administrative roles only.
• The system shall generate alerts in the event of audit logging failure or backup
failure.
• The system shall synchronize system time with an approved time source to ensure
accurate audit timestamps.
• Passwords shall be stored using secure one-way hashing and shall never be stored in
plaintext.
• The system shall support secure configuration baselines and removal of default or
unused system accounts.
• The system shall support regular security patching of application and underlying
components in accordance with defined patch management policy.
• The system shall prevent sensitive data (e.g., full bank account numbers) from being
written to system logs.
14.2 Performance, availability, and scalability
• The system shall support peak disbursement, bulk import, and reporting periods without
performance degradation.
• Target response times for common operations (view list, open application, save draft)
should be <= 3 seconds under normal load.
• Availability target should be >= 99.5% per month excluding planned maintenance,
subject to hosting model.
• The system shall support concurrent access by multiple internal users without
performance degradation.
• The system shall support bulk CSV imports of beneficiary records within defined
volume thresholds without timeout.
• Standard operational reports shall execute within defined response time thresholds
under normal system load.
• The system shall support monitoring and alerting for system availability and critical
background jobs.
• Backup processes shall generate automated alerts upon failure.
• Backups shall be automated; disaster recovery shall define RPO/RTO and include
documented and periodically tested restoration procedures.
14.3 Usability, accessibility, and localization
• User interfaces shall be mobile-friendly and usable on low-bandwidth networks,
including resumable uploads and compressed previews.
• The system shall provide clear, user-friendly error messages that do not expose server
internals; technical errors shall be logged for support.
• The system shall support accessibility considerations (readable fonts, keyboard
navigation, contrast, screen-reader labels) where feasible.
• The system shall support configurable date/time formats and can be extended to
support local languages if required.
• The system shall dynamically render user interfaces based on assigned roles and
permissions.
• The system shall provide real-time validation feedback during data entry to reduce
data entry errors.
• The system shall provide progress indicators for long-running operations such as bulk
imports and report generation.
14.4 Data Governance & Quality
• The system shall enforce consistent validation rules across manual entry, CSV import,
and API interfaces.
• The system shall provide duplicate detection mechanisms for beneficiaries and
payees.
• The system shall support exception queues for resolution of flagged data
inconsistencies.
• The system shall prevent deletion of records that are linked to financial transactions.
14.5 Maintainability & Supportability
• The system shall support separate development, testing, and production
environments.
• The system shall allow configuration of forms, workflows, templates, and reports
without vendor code changes.
• The system shall provide documented API interfaces where integrations are
supported.
• The vendor shall provide defined service level agreements (SLAs) for incident
response and resolution.
• The system shall not impose vendor lock-in and shall allow export of core datasets in
open formats.
15. Verification, Acceptance, and Testing
Each requirement shall be verified using one or more of the following methods:
demonstration, functional testing, document review, security testing, and performance
testing. The vendor shall provide a test plan, test cases, and evidence of testing.
The vendor shall support the development, validation, and refinement of detailed
business process flows, data flow diagrams, and related system design artefacts based
on the agreed functional requirements. This shall include collaboration with the client
during implementation and demonstration sessions to ensure that all process flows and
data models accurately reflect operational needs and system functionality.
16. Deliverables expected from the vendor
• Solution architecture and hosting design (including scalability, security controls, DR).
• Configured SMS application and source code (as per procurement terms), including

• API documentation and data import/export templates.
• Deployment and data migration plan.
• Training plan and handover package.
• Support and maintenance SLA proposal (incident response, bug fix timelines,

20. Ownership, Intellectual Property & Sustainability
The solution may be delivered as either a custom-developed system or a
commercial off-the-shelf (COTS/SaaS) product. The following conditions shall apply:
1. Data Ownership
Press Trust shall retain full and exclusive ownership of all data stored, processed, or
generated within the system at all times.
The vendor shall provide the ability to export all core datasets (beneficiaries, awards,
disbursements, monitoring records, audit logs, and reference data) in open,
standard formats (e.g., CSV, Excel) without restriction.
2. Custom-Developed Components
Where the system or any part thereof is custom-developed specifically for Press Trust:
• Press Trust shall receive full ownership rights to the custom-developed
components.
• The vendor shall hand over complete and up-to-date source code.
• The handover shall include database schemas, configuration files,
deployment scripts, and technical documentation.
• The source code shall be structured, documented, and free from hard-coded
credentials.
• Press Trust shall retain unrestricted rights to use, modify, and further develop
the custom-developed components.
3. Commercial Off-the-Shelf (COTS/SaaS) Components
Where the solution includes commercial off-the-shelf components:
• The vendor shall provide Press Trust with appropriate licensing or subscription
rights.
• Press Trust shall retain full ownership of its data.
• The system shall not impose vendor lock-in.
• The vendor shall provide full administrative access required for configuration
and management.
• The vendor shall provide complete documentation and API documentation
where applicable.
• In the event of contract termination, the vendor shall provide full data
extraction support within an agreed transition period.
4. Business Continuity & Exit Support
Upon termination or expiration of the contract:
• The vendor shall provide data export in open formats.
• The vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance.
• The vendor shall ensure no proprietary mechanisms prevent data migration.
